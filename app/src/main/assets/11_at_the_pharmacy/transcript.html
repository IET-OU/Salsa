Allergies

Erica: Hi, I’ve had a very runny nose for a while and my eyes have been watering a lot. I think I may have an allergy. Do you have anything that might help me?

Pharmacist: Possibly. How long have you been suffering from these symptoms?

Erica: For over a week now. And it’s been getting worse.

Pharmacist: Are your eyes swollen or itchy?

Erica: Yes. My eyes have been quite red and uncomfortable.

Pharmacist: Any other symptoms?

Erica: I’ve been feeling quite tired and I’ve had a bit of a headache, but that’s about it.

Pharmacist: And have you been spending a lot of time outside?

Erica: Yes I have. I’ve been going to the park a lot with my daughter because the weather has been so nice.

Pharmacist: Ok, well from what you’ve told me, it looks like you have hay fever, which is a common pollen allergy. We can treat this with some over-the-counter medication. I’ll give you a course of antihistamines to help your symptoms.

Erica: Ok, what are antihistamines?

Pharmacist: An antihistamine is a drug that reduces itching and swelling. They are good for things like insect bites and allergic reactions.

Erica: How do I take them? Are they tablets?

Pharmacist: Yes, normally they are tablets but they can also come in a nasal spray.

Erica: Are there any side effects?

Pharmacist: Not really. Sometimes people feel drowsy. If you feel drowsy, let us know and we will try a different medication.

Erica: Thanks. Hopefully I’ll be fine.

Pharmacist: Ok, so I will give you these tablets to take twice a day. If they don’t improve your symptoms, I suggest you make an appointment with your GP. You may need a prescription for stronger medication, like steroids.

Erica: Thanks, I’ll try these tablets out first.
