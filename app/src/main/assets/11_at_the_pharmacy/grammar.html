Grammar

Present perfect continuous

There is a tense in English that we use to talk about a continuous action that:

  • Started in the past
  • Is still continuing in the present OR has recently stopped
  • Has a connection with the present time.

This tense is called the present perfect continuous.

Pharmacist: Have you been spending time outside?

Erica: Yes, I’ve been going to the park a lot.

To form the present perfect continuous, take the present tense of have + been + present participle (verb+ing).

It’s been getting worse.

My eyes have been watering.
