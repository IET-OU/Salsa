Vocabulary

an allergy (n)

When your body has a bad response to something, like a particular kind of food, plant or animal.

I have a nut allergy so I have to be very careful with what I eat.

a symptom (n)

The effect of an illness or allergy, like coughing, sneezing, a headache, sore throat etc.

The symptoms of hay fever are a runny nose and red, itchy, watery eyes.

itchy (adj)

When a part of your body, often the skin, gives an uncomfortable feeling that makes you want to scratch it.

I have been scratching my nose all day because it’s so itchy.

swollen (adj)

When a part of your body has become larger and redder because of infection, illness or allergy.

My eyes are red and swollen because of my hay fever.

pollen (n)

The tiny, yellow dust that comes from flowers in the summer.

When there is lots of pollen in the air, people suffer from allergies.

over-the-counter (adj/adv)

When you can buy a medication at the pharmacy without a prescription from your doctor.

I didn’t need a prescription for my hay fever medication. I just got the tablets over-the-counter.

a side effect (n)

An effect from a medication that is unwanted.

This hay fever medication really helps with my symptoms, but it has the side effect of making me feel drowsy.

drowsy (adj)

The feeling of being sleepy and tired.

I felt so drowsy that I didn’t go out. I just went to sleep.

GP (General Practitioner) (n)

A local doctor, not usually in a hospital, who an treat minor illnesses or refer you to other medical care.

a prescription (n)

A note from a doctor that says you can have a particular medication.

The over-the-counter medication didn’t work, so my doctor gave me a prescription for steroids.

a steroid (n)

Powerful medication used to treat particular medical conditions.

The doctor prescribed a course of steroids for my hay fever because the antihistamines didn’t improve my symptoms.
