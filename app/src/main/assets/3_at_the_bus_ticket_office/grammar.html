Grammar

Conditional with 'would' (Conditional 2)

Read the dialogue again. Look for the sentences with if + would.

e.g. "If you left home at 2pm you would miss the connecting bus."

This is called Conditional 2. In these sentences we talk about the results of things that are not likely to happen.

"If you left home at 2pm" = Event (that might not happen)

"You would miss the connecting bus" = Result

In this case, the event is unlikely because we haven't decided when to leave home yet.

Even though we are thinking about the present or the future, we form Conditional 2 with simple past... would + infinitive.
