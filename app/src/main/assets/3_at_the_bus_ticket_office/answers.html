Grammar

Practice: conditional with would

  1. If I took the number 1 bus, it would be faster.
  2. If I bought the tickets now, it would be cheaper.
  3. If my flight was delayed, it would be tight.

Conditional 1 vs conditional 2

The speaker in the second sentence uses Conditional 2 (simple past + would + infinitve) because the event is more unlikely to happen.

Language tips

Stress

  1. Will you make the bus if it's at 19:05 (or will you miss it)?
  2. If you can't get the bus at 18:05, can you get the next one (the one at 19:05)?
  1. I must not miss that bus.
  2. I can't miss that bus, but somebody else can.
  3. I can't miss that bus, but I can miss another one.
