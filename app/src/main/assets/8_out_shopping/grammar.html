Grammar

Word order

When what, where, who, how, when and why introduce questions, they change the order of the subject and verb. The verb comes after the question word.

What is the problem?

How much is it?

But remember: when, what, where, who, how and why are not always questions.

Tell me what the problem is.

I don’t know how much it is.

These examples are not questions. They are statements. The subject stays before the verb.

It is a very common mistake in English to say things like:

Wrong: Can you tell me where is the station, please?

Correct: Can you tell me where the station is, please?


Wrong: I want to know who is that man.

Correct: I want to know who that man is.

So remember, when you speak English, the verb is not always the first thing after when, what, where, who, how and why. Sometimes the verb is later in the sentence, or even right at the end of the sentence!

Practice

Can you correct these sentences in your head?

  1. I asked the girl at the till why could I not get a refund.
  2. She told us how could we get store credit.
  3. She explained to me why was I only allowed to get store credit, not a full refund.
  4. Then I saw why was the girl at the till giving me forms.
  5. I found out where was the customer service desk.
  6. I don’t know what is the best thing to do.
