In this activity, Erica needs to return a jumper to the shop because she doesn’t like the fit. She wants her money back, but there’s a problem.

You will learn some useful vocabulary and expressions for interactions in a shop. You will practise word order and learn some interesting things about politeness and saying no.

Get started

Listen to interactions between customers and shop assistants. What are the words and phrases that they use that make the interactions feel natural and ‘English’?

Here are some examples:

Perfect, thanks.

That’s great, cheers.

No, I’m alright for now.

Here you go.

Do any surprise you? Are there any you don’t understand? Do they seem strange to you? If they do, write them down and ask someone what they mean.
