Vocabulary

a bargain (n)

Something that is surprisingly cheap, good value, inexpensive.

That jumper is only £15. It’s a bargain!

a till (n)

The place in the shop where you go to pay, the machine that handles the money when you pay.

to get a refund (n/v)

When you return an item to the shop and get all your money back.

I didn’t like the jumper that I bought so I took it back to the shop and they gave me a full refund.

to exchange (n/v)

To swap, give something back and receive something else.

The jumper I bought was too big, so I exchanged it for a smaller one.

faulty (adj)

Broken, something wrong with it, not right.

I bought this item yesterday but it’s faulty – there is a hole in it. I would like my money back please.

a tag (n)

The bits of paper on your item that have the product information on, like the price. You take them off before you use the item.

Oh no! I took all the labels off my jumper. Can I still take it back to the shop?

a store credit note (n)

When the shop gives you a voucher or note to spend in their shop, instead of giving you your money back.

a voucher (n)

A piece of paper that you can use like money in a certain shop or for a certain product.

They gave me a £15 voucher for their shop. I can spend it there, but not in another shop.

a transaction (n)

The process of buying and selling something.

Something went wrong with the till when I was paying for my jumper. We had to do the transaction again.

to sign up for something (v)

To give your details, become a member of something, register.

They tried to make me sign up for their loyalty card, but I said no.

a loyalty card (n)

A card from a shop that will give you a discount if you spend a lot of money.

With our shop’s loyalty card you will save 10% if you spend more than £50.

to decline something (v)

To say no to something, to turn it down.

I declined the offer of a loyalty card.
