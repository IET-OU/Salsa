Returning an item

I saw a jumper in a shop, when I was out with Maria, that I really liked. It was a bargain as well. I had no time to try it on so I decided to take a risk and just buy it!

But when I got it home, I tried it on and actually, I was disappointed. It didn’t look good on me because the fit wasn’t quite right, “That’s it,” I said, “I’m taking it back to the shop.”

“Hi, could I return this jumper please.” I said to the girl at the till.

“Would you like a refund or exchange?” she asked.

“I’d like a refund please.”

“What’s the problem? Is it faulty or damaged?”

“No, it’s fine. I just don't like the fit.”

“And have you got the receipt and the tags?”

“The receipt?” I said. “Yes I have. Just a second.” Fortunately I found it at the bottom of my bag. Then there was a problem.

“Oh, I’ve just noticed that you bought this item in the sale,” she said. “I’m afraid we don’t offer refunds on sale items.”

‘Oh no!’ I thought. ‘Just my luck. The one time I take a risk with buying clothes and they won’t let me return it!’

“Can you tell me what I can do then?” I asked. “Can I exchange it?”

“Yes I can let you exchange it for another item,” she said. “Or I can give you store credit. I can give you a voucher for the value of your jumper and you can spend it at the store any time.”

“Ok. I’m short on time now. Is it alright if I take the store credit?” I asked.

“Of course, I’ll just put that transaction through the machine. Is there anything else I can help you with today?”

I said no.

“Can I interest you in signing up for a loyalty card so you receive all our best offers?”

She passed some forms to me. I looked at them. ‘I know what this is,’ I thought. ‘It’s one of those things where I give them all my details so they can send me emails every week. I don’t want that.’

I declined. “I'm alright, but thank you,” I said.

“Are you sure? It won’t take a minute.”

“Sorry, thanks, but I’m afraid I’m not interested”.
