Vocabulary

to join (a class, a group) (v)

To become part of a class, start being a member of a class

There's someone new in the class. He joined yesterday.

What/How about you?

A natural way to return a question to someone or ask someone's opinion.

A: "What do you do for work?"
B: "I'm a mechanic. What about you?"
A: "I'm a student."

a mechanic (n)

A person whose job it is to fix cars or other vehicles.

I'm good with cars and engines. I work as a mechanic.

an engineer (n)

A qualified, specialist person who designs, builds or fixes things like machines, buildings, bridges or electronic devices. He or she has a degree in engineering.

to get a degree

To get the qualification you receive when you finish university

I studied for 3 years at the Open University and got a degree in economics.

What are you up to?

To do, be occupied with, spend your time doing

Q: "What are you up to this week?"
A: "I'm busy looking after my daughter."

right?

A bit like a question tag. You use 'right' to invite someone to agree with you.

We're taking a really good English course, right?

obviously

Clearly, easily understood.

I have a baby so obviously I have to look after her.

A preposition of time meaning "up to a certain time."
