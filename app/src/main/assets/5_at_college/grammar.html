Grammar

Question tags

Read or listen to the dialogue again. There are several question tags.

A question tag is a short question that we can add at the end of a statement. They appear most often in spoken English.

You're Andrei, aren't you?

It seems like a good course, doesn't it?

I'll see you next week, won't I?

Forming question tags is easy. If there is one, use the auxiliary (be or have) or modal verb (can, must, would, should etc.).

Weather’s been terrible, hasn’t it?

If there isn't, make the tag with do.

It seems like a good course, doesn't it?

A positive statement has a negative tag.

You joined our class this week, didn't you?

A negative statement has a positive tag.

Yeah, it can't be easy with a little one, can it?

Listen to the intonation of these tags. When the tag questions are really questions, the voice goes up at the end.

But when the tag question is not really a question, the voice goes down at the end.

Often, question tags are not really questions that expect an answer. They are ways of inviting the other person to comment and so to keep the conversation open.
