Vocabulary

to pass by (v)

To travel past somewhere, maybe by chance.

I was passing by the library so, while I was near, I went inside to ask for information.

to drop in (v)

To make an informal visit - can be unplanned or for a short time.

I dropped in to see a friend while I was in town.

to enquire about something (v)

To ask for information.

I went to the library to enquire about joining.

to register for something (v)

To join, to sign up, to give your personal information so you can become a member.

It’s easy to register at the library. You just need to bring proof of address and then fill out the registration form.

a proof of address (n)

A document that shows what your address is.

When you register, you need to bring a letter from your bank as proof of address.

to borrow something (v)

To have something for a short time and then give it back.

At the library you can borrow books, CDs and DVDs for two weeks.

an item (n)

A thing, an object.

You can borrow a maximum of 5 items a day from the library, such as books, CDs and DVDs.

a catalogue (n)

A collection of items.

I looked through the library catalogue and I found the book that I wanted.

a session (n)

A period of time arranged for a particular activity.

There is a session on a Tuesday morning especially for young children.

a nursery rhyme (n)

A traditional song or poem for young children.

At the children session we all sang nursery rhymes. I knew all the words because they are well-known songs.

a charge (n)

A price for an item or a service.

There is a charge of £1.30 per half hour for using the computers at the library.
