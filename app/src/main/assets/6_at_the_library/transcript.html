Activities at the library

I was passing by the library on Wednesday and I thought I would drop in to enquire about joining. I am interested in joining the library because it will help with learning English and because I hear the library has activities for little ones like Maria.

“Good morning,” I said to the woman at reception. “I was just passing by and I was wondering how I could join the library. What do I need to do?”

“Of course,” she said. “We always welcome new members. To register we will need to see some ID and proof of address from the last 3 months.”

I said, “Ok, I can do that. I had to do that when I registered on my ESOL course as well.”

“Oh, you’re on an ESOL course, are you?” she asked. “Then you don’t need to give us ID again. You just need to give us the confirmation letter from your course.”

“So you mean I don’t have to bring in my ID or proof of address? Is that right?” I asked. “I just need to bring the confirmation letter?”

She said that was right.

“Great”, I said. “I don’t have it now, but I can bring it by the end of the week.” Then I wanted to know more. “Could I ask you, what do I get with my membership?” I enquired. “What services can I use?”

“Well,” she explained. “Obviously you can borrow and reserve items from the library catalogue. You might also be interested in our IT services, including printing, photocopying and scanning. You can use our computers and WiFi as well.”

“Fantastic,” I said. “That sounds really good because I don’t have a computer or anything like that at home. Oh, I also wanted to ask - doesn’t the library offer activities for families and small children? I have a daughter who is nearly two.”

“Yes we do indeed,” she said with a smile. “We have a lovely session of nursery rhymes and songs on Tuesdays from 10am. There are lots of activities for older children as well.”

“That’s wonderful,” I said. “She will really like the songs. Oh, and can I just check – is it free to join?”

“Yes, it’s free to join,” she said. “It’s also free to borrow and renew items, and you have half an hour of free wifi every day. There is a charge for other services like computing, but it’s not expensive, don’t worry!”

“That’s great,” I said, relieved. “So I don’t need to bring money when I come back?”

“Not at all,” she said. “You and your daughter can go to the baby sessions completely free.”
