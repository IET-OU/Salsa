Using the pool

Receptionist: Right, so thank you for visiting us. Let me take you through using the pool. There is a swimming class every morning, which means you can’t swim unless you are taking lessons.

Erica: OK, so you mean I can only swim if I come in the afternoon.

Receptionist: That’s right. It’s recommended that you come around lunchtime or in the evening for public swimming.

Erica: And do you have something suitable for toddlers? My daughter really likes water.

Receptionist: Yes that’s fine, we have a toddler pool and she can come as long as she stays in it. Armbands and non-swimmers aren’t allowed in the main pool.

Erica: That’s ok. As long as she’s in the water, she’ll be happy!

Receptionist: Great. So, before using the pool, all swimmers are asked to use the showers.

Erica: Fine. And what about changing?

Receptionist: There are lots of options. You can get changed in the individual cubicles or, if you come with your daughter, the bigger family cubicles in the women’s changing rooms. Your belongings and valuables can be stored in the lockers once you’re ready to swim.

Erica: Thanks. That seems simple. So I want to come every week, I think. Can you tell me what the available discounts are? Is there a season ticket? Or maybe a family ticket?

Receptionist: I’m afraid family tickets are for four people or more, but you can buy a season pass that will give you free, unlimited access and one free session per month, anywhere in the leisure centre.

Erica: Great! I like the sound of that. What other activities can I do here?

Receptionist: Oh there’s a lot going on here: we have a gym, and offer fitness, aerobics, yoga, salsa classes…

Erica: Salsa dancing! I’ve always wanted to try salsa. So I get to do that free once a month?

Receptionist: Yes, as long as you buy your season ticket.

Erica: Oh this is perfect – I hope the ticket isn’t too pricey!
