Language tips

Using passives

Look at the conversation again. Notice where the receptionist at the leisure centre uses passive forms.

Non-swimmers aren’t allowed in the main pool.

Why do you think this is?

In a passive sentence, the object of the action becomes the subject of the sentence.

We use the passive because the main topic (the thing we are most interested in) normally comes at the beginning of the sentence.

Valuables can be stored in lockers.

Season tickets can be bought at reception.

In these examples we don’t know exactly who does the action, or we don’t care who does it.

It is particularly common to use passives when we are being formal. In the examples we use passives because they are indirect and impersonal.

In the dialogue, the receptionist uses many passive constructions because she is more formal. Erica uses none.

It’s recommended that you come around lunchtime.

All swimmers are asked to use the showers first.

Non-swimmers aren’t allowed in the main pool.

Valuables can be stored in lockers.

Phrasal verbs using get

Get to do is a phrasal verb with get that Erica uses in the dialogue

get to do something

To be allowed to do something. To be given the opportunity to do something.

Because I bought a season ticket I got to try out salsa dancing.

There are lots of other phrasal verbs with get in English. Here are a few more to practise.

get up to

To do, spend your time doing.

Q: What do you get up to when you go to the leisure centre?
A: I mostly go swimming.

get over something

To recover from something.

I can’t do salsa classes because I’m still getting over my ankle injury.

get away with something

To escape blame or punishment.

I was late back from lunch but I got away with it. My boss didn’t notice!

get rid of something

To remove or throw away something that you don’t want.

I didn’t like my swimming trunks so I got rid of them.

get out of something

To avoid doing something that you don’t want to do.

I got out of paying full price for my ticket by telling them that I’m a student.
