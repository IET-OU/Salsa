Vocabulary

to take somebody through something (v)

To explain something to someone, to tell someone about a process or something that happened.

He took me through how to fill in the form for my season ticket.

a toddler (n)

A small child who is just beginning to walk.

My daughter is only 2 years old. She is just a toddler.

a cubicle (n)

A small room within a room, for example one with a shower or toilet. It could also be a place to change your clothes.

Before your swim, you can get changed in an individual cubicle or a bigger family cubicle.

belongings (n)

Personal items that belong to you, things that are yours.

I put all my belongings in the locker while I went for a swim.

valuables (n)

Things that are yours that you want to keep safe because they are expensive – like your wallet and phone.

I didn’t want someone to steal my phone or other valuables so I put them in the locker.

a locker (n)

A cupboard to keep your clothes or valuables in. You can lock the cupboard to keep your things safe.

Put your valuables in a locker while you swim. Make sure you look after the key!

unlimited access to something (n)

You can use something as much as you want, whenever you want. You don’t pay any more.

A season ticket gives you unlimited access to the swimming pool.

a session (n)

A period of time arranged for a particular activity.

There is a session at the pool every Wednesday that’s for toddlers.

leisure (n)

The time when you are not working/your free time, e.g. when you are playing sport, exercising, reading or walking.

A leisure centre is a place where you can enjoy your free time with activities like swimming, sports, dance classes or using the gym or sauna.

to get to do something (v)

To be given the opportunity to do something.

Because I bought a season ticket I got to try out salsa dancing.

pricey (adj)

A colloquial way to say that something is expensive.

I wanted to buy a season ticket to the leisure centre but I couldn’t afford it. It was a bit pricey.
