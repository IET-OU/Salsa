Language Learning strategies/tips on the bus

(1) Live Eavesdropping!

Pay close attention to a conversation behind you. Don't try and understand EVERY word. What's the topic? Write down/note...

  • 2 ways that show the speaker's opinion/feelings
  • 2 ways that show the listener's opinion/feelings
  • 2 noises you hear the speakers making
  • 2 expressions you want to understand better

(2) Mobile Eavesdropping!

Pay close attention to a mobile phone conversation behind you. Try and work out what the absent person is saying? Write down/note...

  • what subjects they talk about
  • what their relationship is (how do you know? what clues are there?)
  • what emotions you can hear (how do you know?)
  • any expressions you want to understand better

(3) Language Capture!

Pay close attention to any signs/notices around you. Try and work out what they mean. Take photographs of the notices to research later and write down/note...

  • new vocabulary/expressions you can guess the meaning of
  • any questions you have about language or culture

(4) Small talk!

Try and sit near or next to a friendly looking person (tip; try an elderly woman) and start a conversation

  • you can start with by asking about the next stop/or the bus route
  • if this goes well, make a remark about the weather
  • nod, smile and make encouraging noises ("Hmm." "Uh huh") even if you don't understand
  • Remember, if you feel shy or afraid, the only way to progress on your language journey is to talk to people. "Feal the fear and do it anyway!"

Small talk at the bus stop

  • "Number 7? It's just gone. You've missed it"
  • "Another one will be along in a minute"
  • "I've been waiting for ages..."
  • "(What a) lovely day!"
  • "(What) miserable weather!"
  • "It's freezing today, (isn't it)?"
  • "Looks like it might rain/turn out nice!"
  • "It's not a very nice day!"
  • "It's clearing up!"
  • "(It's a) bit nippy!"

(response; smile/nod/"hmmm" animation with sound?)

Queuing etiquette

  • Queues and queuing behaviour is important.
  • Don't queue jump, or push in!
  • Line up or stand next to or behind the people already there.
  • Ask "Is this the back of the queue?"
  • Or "Where does the queue start?"
  • Don't invite friends to jump ahead and join you.
  • Don't have your music so loud others can hear it.
  • Say "After you!" and allow elderly, disabled or mothers with children to board before you.
  • You will need coins, or change, not big notes
  • Don't use your phone while talking to the bus driver

'On board' bus etiquette

  • Always say "please" after asking for your ticket.
  • Give up priority seats to those less able to stand.
  • Take your litter with you if there is no bin.
  • Smelly food & hot drinks are not welcomed.
  • Loud music or conversations are not welcome.
  • On crowded buses, don't put your bag on the seat beside you.
  • To get out of a seat past your neighbour say "Excuse me, I'm getting off here".
  • Say "excuse me!" or "sorry!' as you go past people to get to the front.
  • Always say "thank you" to the driver as you get off.
  • Or "thanks", "thanks a lot!" "cheers!".

Travelling by bus vocabulary

  • to catch the/a bus
  • to miss the/a bus
  • to get on the/a bus
  • to get off the/a bus
  • to hop on the/a bus
  • to hop off the/a bus
  • to go there/come back by bus (a/the)
  • to sit at the back of the bus
  • to sit in the front of the bus
  • to be stuck in traffic/a pile up/a traffic jam

Time and Travel on the bus

  • "When's the next 6A?"
  • "There's one due at quarter past"
  • "I's running (a bit) late/on time"
  • "It's been delayed/cancelled"
  • "Can I buy a ticket on the bus?"
  • "Does it/Do you stop at Sainsbury's?"
  • "Could you tell me when we get to the station, please?"
  • "What's this/the next/last stop, please?"
  • "I'm getting off here/at the next stop/in a minute"
  • "Ooops! I should have got off at the last stop!"

Bus vocabulary

  • A single/double decker bus
  • Bus fare "Please have the correct fare ready"
  • A single/return/all-day/season ticket
  • A (student/concession) travel card
  • An aisle seat/ a window seat
  • CCTV cameras "Smile! You're on camera!"
  • bus route
  • bus lane
  • bus strike
  • bus timetable/bus times

Useful bus travel language

  • "Can I have a single/return/all day (ticket) to..., please?"
  • "Am I on the right bus for (Woughton Park)?"
  • "Do you mind if I sit here/open the window?"
  • "Not at all/Go ahead/Please do" = Yes sit down
  • "Actually, I'm a bit cold/There's a bit of a breeze" = No, don't open the window!
  • "Have we gone past the city centre?"
  • "Excuse me, when is/is this the last/first bus (to..)?
  • "Would you like a seat? /Please, take a seat!
  • "Is this seat free?" = Please move your bag so I can sit down.
  • "Sorry, is it too loud? I'll turn the volume down!"
