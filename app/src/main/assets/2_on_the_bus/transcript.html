Finding your way

Getting around the UK can be so frustrating sometimes. Today I tried to visit a friend of a friend from back home. She had offered to help us settle in to Milton Keynes. She said we could take the bus but I wasn't sure which route to take. When a bus came along, I got on through the front door with the pram so I could ask the driver for help.

"Excuse me", I said, "I would like to get to Broughton. Is this the right bus?"

"'Fraid not," said the bus driver, "You want to get on the hospital bus to get to Wroughton."

"To the hospital? That can't be right." I said. I thought that maybe I was pronouncing the name wrong. So I tried again.

"Sorry," I said, “perhaps I meant Broughton."

"Loughton?" he replied. "No, Loughton's over by the railway station. I'm going the other way."

'What? But I know where the railway station is!' I thought. I told him that I didn't want to go to that way.

"Hang on one moment," I said, and I looked in my handbag. I managed to find a piece of paper with the woman's address on. I showed it to the driver. "Look, I want to go here, Broughton. Do you know where it is?"

"Oh, you mean Broughton, do you?" said the driver.

"I don't know. Do I?" I replied. "I'm confused. I have no idea any more!"

"Is it over by the motorway?" asked the driver.

"Yes that's it!" I said. "She told me she lives near the motorway."

"That'll be Broughton then," said the bus driver. "You had me confused there. You see," he went on, "we've got Woughton, Loughton, and Broughton and they're all spelled the same, just pronounced differently."

“Oh, I see,” I said. I finally realised what the problem was. “You thought you were confused – what about me?! I’m new here. How am I supposed to know that?”

“You’ll learn, don’t worry,” he laughed. “If you’re not sure, just ask.”

“Thanks. That’s very kind. So how much is a ticket to... where am I going again?"

"Broughton."

"Broughton! How much is a ticket to Broughton?"

"£2.20 for a single, or £4.20 for a day ticket", he said.

I told him I would just buy a single.
