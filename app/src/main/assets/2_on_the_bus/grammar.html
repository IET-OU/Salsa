3 Grammar

Tenses and reported speech

Reported speech is when we talk about something that somebody said in the past. We usually change the tense of the verb.

There are lots of examples of reported speech in Erica's story.

  • She said we could take the bus.
  • I told him I would just buy a single.

What were the words that were actually said?

  • We can take the bus
  • I will just buy a single.

Now read the story again and look for more examples of reported speech. Can you find them all?

Answers

  • She said we could take the bus.
  • I told him I didn't want to go that way.
  • I thought I was pronouncing the name wrong.
  • I told him I would just buy a single.

Look at the way she uses tenses.

  • can --> could
  • don't want --> didn't want
  • am pronouncing --> was pronouncing
  • will --> would

When you report speech, you usually use a tense that is 'more past' than the words that were actually said. For example:

  • present simple --> past simple
  • past simple --> past perfect
  • present perfect --> past perfect

Practice

Try to turn these sentences into reported speech. Pay attention to verb tenses. The answers are at the bottom of the page.

Example

I understand the bus driver. --> I said I understood the bus driver.

  1. I know which bus to take.
  2. I have had a bad day.
  3. I am really struggling with the pram.
  4. I will take the bus to the hospital instead.
  5. I can't get to your house.

Answers

  1. I said I knew which bus to take.
  2. I said I had had a bad day.
  3. I said I was really struggling with the pram.
  4. I said I would take the bus to the hospital instead.
  5. I said I couldn't get to her house.
