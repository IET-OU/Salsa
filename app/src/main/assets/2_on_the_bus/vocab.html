The next day, Erica does take the bus to see her friend. She travels with her one-year-old daughter. Here are some words that would be useful for her on her journey.

2 Vocabulary

rear doors (n)

The doors at the back of the bus.

Please get on the bus using the rear doors.

aisle (n)

The space in the middle of the bus that you walk down.

I had to stand in the aisle because there were no free seats.

peak time (n)

The time of day when public transport is busiest, usually at the beginning and the end of the working day.

At peak times, bus tickets are more expensive.

off peak (adj)

The time when public transport isn't very busy, usually in the middle of the day or late in the evening.

Older persons can travel free when it's off peak.

valid ticket (adj)

A correct ticket that you are able to use. You have paid the correct price for the correct date.

I'm afraid that ticket isn't valid for this journey. You will have to buy another one.

eligible for a discount (adj)

You are allowed to save money when buying a ticket or a bus pass. Maybe because you are old, disabled or very young.

Your daughter is eligible for a discount on her ticket because she is under five years old.

priority seating (n)

Seats on the bus that are for old or disabled people, or for people with small children.

give up your seat (v)

To give your seat to somebody else.

He gave up his seat to the woman with the baby.

pram / buggy (n)

A vehicle with wheels to push a child in. A pram is for babies and a buggy is for larger children.

She put the pram next to the priority seating because there was enough space for it.
