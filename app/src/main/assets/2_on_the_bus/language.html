4 Language tips

Indirect language

English people are well known for being very indirect when they speak. Sometimes people say things but mean something a bit stronger!

Here are some strange ways of speaking that you might hear on the bus. See if you can match the phrases with their 'translations'.

  1. "Excuse me, sorry, is this seat free?"
  2. "Sorry, do you mind if I sit here?"
  3. "Could you maybe help me with my buggy?"
  4. "I'm not sure that's the right way."
  5. "I think you might have dropped something."
  6. "I think you might be sitting in my seat."
  7. "Would you mind if I just squeeze past you?"
  1. That's my seat and I want to sit there.
  2. Get out of my way please, I want to get past.
  3. Is anyone going to help me because I'm having real problems?
  4. You have definitely just dropped your ticket in front of me.
  5. Move your bag please because I want to sit in that seat!
  6. That's completely the wrong way!
  7. I am going to sit here, whether you like it or not!

Answers

  1. E
  2. G
  3. C
  4. F
  5. D
  6. A
  7. B
