4 Language tips

Stress

Stress plays an important role in spoken English. Sometimes changing the stressed word can change the meaning.

Listen to these two questions. Can you hear what the difference is

The bus is at 19:05. Will you make that?

The next bus is at 19:05. Will you make that?

Answer

  1. Will you make the bus if it's at 19:05 (or will you miss it)?
  2. If you can't get the bus at 18:05, can you get the next one (the one at 19:05)?

Here is another example. What are the meanings of these sentences with the stress placed on different words?

  1. "No I can't miss that one!"
  2. "I can't miss that one!"
  3. "No I can't miss that one!"

Answers

  1. I must not miss that bus.
  2. I can't miss that bus, but somebody else can.
  3. I can't miss that bus, but I can miss another one.
