2 Vocabulary

counter (n)

The place in a café or shop where you order something.

In the UK, you normally order your coffee at the counter, not at your table.

complimentary (adj)

free, given to you without cost

You get one hour of complimentary wifi in this café

room (n)

space, area, place, NOT like 'living room', 'bathroom' etc.

There was no room in the café for the pram, so I left it outside.

squeeze (v)

manage to pass through a small space

Erica squeezed through the chairs with her pram.

reach (v)

arrive at, get to.

Erica reached her table once the man had moved his chair.

hand (v)

to give something to someone (by hand)

She handed Erica a piece of paper with the wifi password on.

lowercase (adj)

text without capital letters.

Remember that the wifi password is all lowercase. Don't use capital letters.

be off (v)

to go, leave

I'm off now. Bye!

lovely (adj)

nice, pleasant

It's been lovely meeting you!
