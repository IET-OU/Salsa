3 Grammar

Past perfect

Read or listen to the text again. Notice the use of verb tenses like this:

“I remembered I had forgotten to get the wifi password.”

“The girl had already written it down for me.”

“Maria had not woken up.”

This tense is called the past perfect. It is formed with the past simple of have - had + past participle.

When we talk about an event that happened in the past we use the past simple (I remembered). When we talk about an event that happened before that time, we need to use the past perfect (had forgotten). For example:

Tense: Past perfect
Form: had + past participle
Use: To say which of two past actions or events happened first/An action in the past completed before another action in the past.

Often, the past perfect appears with 'already'.

“The girl had already written it down for me.”

Practice

Complete the sentence with the correct form of the past perfect. Think about why the past perfect is being used.Answers are at the bottom.

Example

  1. The old lady offered to buy me a coffee, but I ___ already ______ (buy) one.
  2. A woman spoke to me in the café whom I ___ never ___ (meet) before.
  3. I had to go home quickly because Maria ___ _____ __ (wake up).
  4. I realised that I ___ ____ (leave) my wallet at home.
  5. I couldn't log into the wifi because I ___ ___ _____ (not ask) for the wifi password.

Answers

  1. The old lady offered to buy me a coffee, but I had already bought one.
  2. A woman spoke to me in the café whom I had never met before.
  3. I had to go home quickly because Maria had woken up.
  4. I realised that I had left my wallet at home.
  5. I couldn't log into the wifi because I had not asked for the wifi password.

The actions in the past perfect all happen before the actions in the past simple.
