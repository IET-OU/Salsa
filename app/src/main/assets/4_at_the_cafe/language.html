Language tips

Patterns of conversation

Read Erica's conversation with the old woman again. Think about the pattern of the conversation.

Does it go like this?

Question-Answer-Question-Answer

No. A conversation is not normally like that. Social conversations have a more varied structure.

Erica's conversation has a structure more like this:

ASK-ANSWER-ADD-ASK

Old lady: "What's her name?" - ASK

Erica: "Her name is Maria" - ANSWER

Old lady: "She's adorable! She looks just like you. - ADD

Erica: "Thank you. Do you have children yourself?" - ASK

Think about how you can maintain conversations in English with patterns like this. This way, both people ask questions and both people answer questions.

Icebreakers

To break the ice is an expression in English that means to start a conversation with someone that you don't know.

In Erica's story, the old woman 'breaks the ice' by talking about Erica's baby.

There are lots of ways to start a conversation, but here are a few useful phrases.

Do you mind if I join you?


How's it going?


How has your day been?


Are you from around here?


I was wondering if you could help me.


Do you come here often?


Do you know if...?

And of course, English people love talking about the weather so you can always start with...

Terrible weather today, isn't it!?
