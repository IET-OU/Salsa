4 Language tips

Politeness

English people are often very indirect when they speak as a way of being polite. In formal conversations, it’s good to use phrases like these when making a request.

Could you…?

Would it be possible to…?

I would quite like to know if…?

Would you be able to…?

Other good phrases to remember:

Sorry to bother you.

Bear with me please.

Practice

Think about the following pairs of sentences. Which shows that the speaker is being polite about making a request?

1A: “Could I report a problem to you?”

1B: “I want you to sort out a problem for me.”

2A: “Good morning, so are you going to do anything about the graffiti on my street”

2B: “Good morning, I’m sorry to bother you but could you help me with a problem on my street.”

3A: “We can’t do it now, sorry.”

3B: “I’m afraid we won’t be able to remove it straight away”

4A: “Bear with me please. Let me take your details.”

4B: “Hold on, I need your details now.”

Answers

  1. A
  2. B
  3. B
  4. A
