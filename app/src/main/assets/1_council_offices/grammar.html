3 Grammar

Phrasal verbs

Phrasal verbs are made up of a verb and a particle (little word e.g 'up'). Sometimes two particles. There are a lot of phrasal verbs in English and it is very difficult to learn them all. Sometimes it is easy to guess the meaning of phrasal verbs, sometimes it is impossible!

It is important to know that there are different types of phrasal verb that work in different ways.

There are three phrasal verbs in the dialogue:

  • Follow up
  • Go about
  • Put up with

Separable phrasal verbs

Follow up is a separable phrasal verb. This means that the verb and the particle can be separated in the sentence. The meaning is the same.

The council will follow up the complaint.

The council will follow the complaint up.

However, if there is an object pronoun like it, the pronoun must go between the verb and the particle.

The council will follow it up.

Inseparable phrasal verbs

Go about is an example of an inseparable phrasal verb. The verb and the particle must always be together in the sentence.

How do I go about making a complaint?
NOT How do I go making a complaint about?

Some phrasal verbs, like put up with have two particles. These particles can never be separated from the verb and must always appear in the same order.

Must I put up with all this graffiti?
NOT Must I put with all this graffiti up?

Practice

Fill in the gaps in the sentence with option A, B or C. You can do this in your head. The answers are below.

  1. There was a report of graffiti made by a local resident but nobody _______. (A: went about it B: followed it up C: followed up it)
  2. I wanted to make a complaint to the council but I had no idea how to _______ it. (A: go about B: put up with C: follow up)
  3. Erica had to _______ the graffiti in her neighbourhood for several weeks. (A: follow up B: put up with C: go about)
  4. Do you mind _______ for a few more days until we have the time to remove it? (A: putting up it with B: putting with it up C: putting up with it)
  5. I just wanted to know if anyone has _______ on my message from last week. (A: followed up - B: gone about - C: followed it up)

Answers

  1. B: There was a report of graffiti made by a local resident but nobody followed it up.
  2. A: I wanted to make a complaint to the council but I had no idea how to go about it.
  3. B: Erica had to put up with the graffiti in her neighbourhood for several weeks.
  4. C: Do you mind putting up with it for a few more days until we have the time to remove it?
  5. A: I just wanted to know if anyone has followed up on my message from last week.
