2 Vocabulary

graffiti (n)

writing or drawings painted illegally on walls or buildings

The police caught the young men drawing graffiti all over the bus stop.

bother (v)

cause a problem, trouble or be annoying to someone

Sorry to bother you, but could you help me.

report (v)

to inform or tell someone about something formally

You should report any offensive graffiti in your neighbourhood to the council.

go about (v)

to start an activity

The council quickly went about removing the graffiti.

complaint (n)

a report of something that is bad or unsatisfactory

I would like to make a complaint about graffiti in my neighbourhood.

follow up (v)

to do something about a complaint or report

The council tries to follow up all reports of offensive graffiti.

obscene (adj)

offensive or disgusting, often of a sexual nature

The graffiti was obscene because it used sexual words.

count as (v)

to have a particular quality, to meet a criteria, to amount to being something

It doesn’t count as graffiti if you paint it in your own house.

put up with (v)

tolerate something, accept a situation that you don't like

Erica had to put up with the graffiti until the council was able to remove it.

log (v)

to record or write something down to be used later

The council logged the details of the graffiti on Erica’s street.
