Language tips

Agree to disagree

In the dialogue, Erica and Andrei disagree with each other but they respect each other’s opinions.

They use phrases to say that they disagree, and then they introduce their own opinion.

I’m not sure I agree with that.

I think you’re missing the point.

They also use phrases to accept what the other person has said before expressing their own opinion.

Well that may be so but I still think…

I see what you’re saying, but…

I take your point, but you haven’t convinced me!

Notice how they do not say that the other person is wrong.

In the end, they agree to disagree, which is a pleasant and polite way to end an argument by respecting the other person’s point of view.

Idioms

An idiom is a group of words whose meaning is different from what you would expect. Erica and Andrei use a lot of nice idioms in the dialogue.

Here are the meanings. See if you can find the idiom in the dialogue.

  1. To not understand.
  2. To accept each other’s different opinions.
  3. To not do something, to not go to an event.
  4. It’s not something I like or am interested in.
