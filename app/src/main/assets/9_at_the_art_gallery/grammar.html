Grammar

Forming the passive

In passive sentences, the object of the action becomes the subject of the sentence. We choose active or passive depending on what we are most interested in.

Active: Some of the landscapes really impress me.

Passive: I am really impressed by some of the landscapes.

In this sentence, I is the subject of the sentence, but is also the person that is affected by the verb impressed. Landscapes are doing the action (impressing) but they are not the subject.

Forming the passive is very simple. It is formed using the right person and tense of to be + past participle.

Present simple: I am fascinated by these portraits.

Past simple: He was bored by the art gallery.

Past perfect: The painting had been painted by a talented artist.

Future: A new painting will be brought to the gallery on Monday.

To learn more about how and when to use the passive, see Activity 7.

Practice

Make these active sentences passive. You can do them in your head, and then check your answers.

  1. Everybody loved the painting.
  2. The artist makes his sculptures out of metal.
  3. A hundred people visited the gallery today.
  4. The new exhibition at the gallery will really impress you.
  5. The artist designed the MK Rose to have the structure of a flower.
