Vocabulary

to impress someone (v)

To make someone feel respect, make someone think something is good

The beauty of the paintings really impressed me.

a landscape (n)

a picture of countryside or an outdoor scene.

The painting was a landscape of the countryside outside Milton Keynes.

a portrait (n)

A picture of a person

She painted the most beautiful portrait of a young man.

to see the point of something (v)

To understand the importance, reason or purpose of something

I couldn’t see the point of going to an art gallery because I’m not interested in art.

talented (adj)

have a natural skill or ability for something, for example painting.

The artist was able to paint beautiful paintings that everyone loved. She was so talented.

overrated (adj)

not as good as people say it is

Everyone talks about art like it’s so amazing, but personally I think it’s overrated.

to make up something (v)

To create or invent something that isn’t real

The man was saying that the painting was worth millions of pounds, but I think he was making it up.

to convince someone (v)

To persuade, change someone’s mind, make someone believe something

I didn’t really want to go to the art gallery, but Erica convinced me that I would enjoy it.

to fancy doing something (v) (colloquial)

To feel like doing something, to want to do something

I fancy going to the park to see the sculptures. Do you want to come with me?

a sculpture (n)

a piece of three-dimensional art made out of stone or metal, for example a statue.

Let’s go to Campbell Park. There are some really interesting sculptures there made out of metal. I would like to see them.

a monument (n)

a statue or building to remember an important person or an event.

In Campbell Park there is a monument to remember the two World Wars.
