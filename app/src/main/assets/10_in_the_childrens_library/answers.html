Introduction

Get started

  1. C nursery: a place where young children and babies are taken care of while their parents are at work
  2. A crèche: a place where young children are cared for during the day while their parents do something else, especially work, study, or shop
  3. E playgroup / playschool: an organised group for children aged between three and five to play and learn together in a place outside their homes
  4. B day care: care or education provided during the day, especially for young children
  5. D childminder: a person whose job is to take care of other people's children in her or his own home
  6. F babysitter: someone who looks after children at home while parents go out in the evening

Grammar

Practice: articles

  1. Kids are often like that. They get overexcited.
  2. I saw a child in the park. The child belonged to a woman I know.
  3. It’s great, because there is a health visitor at the playgroup we go to.
  4. The embarrassment was too much for me. I had to leave.
  5. It’s a problem that childcare is so expensive in the United Kingdom.
  6. I know of a good children’s centre near Campbell Park.
  7. I gave Maria a cuddle. Cuddles always stop her crying.
  8. I owed the woman an apology because Maria had broken another toy.

Prepositions of movement

  1. across (from one side to the other side)
  2. after (one follows the other), away (put more distance between)
  3. around (in a circular or repetitive way)
  4. towards (in the direction of something)
  5. down (from high to low)
  6. past (pass nearby to something)
  7. along (moving in a line)
