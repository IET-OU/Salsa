Vocabulary

a rhyme (n)

A traditional song or poem for young children.

At the children session we all sang nursery rhymes. I knew all the words because they are well-known songs.

exciting (adj)

Something that causes someone to be happy, energetic and really enjoy themselves.

Maria was really excited at the playgroup because there were so many other children to play with. She said it was exciting.

to carry on doing something (v)

To continue doing something.

Maria didn’t come when I asked her to. She carried on playing with her friend.

to turn up (v)

To arrive, appear, come

The girl’s mother turned up just as Maria took her toy. I was so embarrassed.

I can tell

I know, see, understand or realise something.

I could tell they were going to be friends because they played so well together.

a playgroup (n)

A place to take children so they can play with each other and take part in activities.

I take Maria to a playgroup every Tuesday and she loves it.

childcare (n)

Care for children, particularly by other people (e.g. in a nursery or crèche).

Childcare is so expensive in this country. I look after Maria myself.

a health visitor (n)

A nurse who goes to homes or childcare centres to give health checkups to children.

The playgroup is great because we can go to the health visitor to get health advice as well.

embarrassed (adj)

Feeling uncomfortable, shy, self-conscious, or ashamed about something.

I was so embarrassed by Maria’s behaviour that my face went red. I apologised to the girl’s mother.

to make a fuss (v)

To be unnecessarily upset or excited about something, to make a big thing out of something.

The girl wasn’t really hurt. She was just making a fuss.

to apologise for something/doing something (v)

To say sorry

I made Maria apologise for taking the girl’s toy.
