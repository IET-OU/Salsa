Language tips

Formal and informal language

The conversation in Erica’s story uses a lot of informal language. It is a friendly, familiar conversation.

What are the features of the conversation that make it informal? First read the story again and think about it yourself.

Informal language

Discourse markers to organise thoughts

“Ok”, “anyway”, “well”, “hey”, “so”

Missing out words (ellipsis)

“Sounds great” (that sounds great), “Really kind of you” (That’s really kind of you), “Happy to help” (I’m happy to help)

Vague language

“great things for toddlers”, “loads of help”

Contracted forms

“I’ll see you next week”, “she’s not usually like that”, “they’re getting on well”

Personal language (e.g. saying ‘you’ a lot)

“They give you loads of help and advice”

Formal language

Words to link clauses, sentences and ideas

Therefore, however, subsequently

Complete sentences without ellipsis

The playgroup sounds interesting, I would be happy to help you

Precise language

They offer some wonderful activities for toddlers, there are many forms of help available

Impersonal language (avoiding 'you' and 'I', using passives)

Help is available from the health visitor, the toy isn’t needed
