Grammar

Articles

Use the exercise below to practise using the articles a, an and the.

First, remember how articles work in English.

a/an

  • for a thing or person that we’re mentioning for the first time
  • when it doesn’t matter which thing we’re talking about

the

  • for a person or thing that we already know about
  • when we’ve already mentioned it before
  • when it is clear which one we mean

no article

to refer to people or things in general

Practice

Fill in the gap with a, an, the or no article. Do it in your head then check your answers.

  1. ______ kids are often like that. They get overexcited.
  2. I saw ____ child in the park. _____ child belonged to a woman I know.
  3. It’s great, because there is _____ health visitor at ____ playgroup we go to.
  4. _____ embarrassment was too much for me. I had to leave.
  5. It’s a problem that ______ childcare is so expensive in _____ United Kingdom.
  6. I know of ______ good children’s centre near _____ Campbell Park.
  7. I gave Maria _____ cuddle. _____ cuddles always stop her crying.
  8. I owed _____ woman _____ apology because Maria had broken another toy.
